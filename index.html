Executive Takeaways

1. Early lifecycle is the critical save window.
Customers in 0-6 month tenure bands have materially higher churn and should receive high-touch onboarding.
2. Contract design is a major retention lever.
Month-to-month subscribers churn much more than annual-plan users; migration campaigns are high ROI.
3. Service support correlates with lower risk.
Security and support add-ons should be bundled for high-risk broadband segments.
4. Billing experience drives churn behavior.
Electronic-check users show elevated churn, indicating payment friction and engagement gaps.
